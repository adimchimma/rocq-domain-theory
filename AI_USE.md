# NOTE on AI Use

## AI was used mainly in three Areas.
- 1: Helping me plan and structure the project. if you look far enough at the git history, you'll see that I had a whole different architecture that ended up not being viable. Gaining an understanding of what I should focus my energy on learning and quirks of rocq that I might encounter was a direct result of asking LLMs questions, as well as reading docs, reports, other libraries, and the relevant textbooks/papers, of course.

- 2: Cleaning up proofs that became too unwieldy. I sometimes would have proofs that got too large, so I would have an LLM help clean it up if I couldn't be bothered to. It would also help write documentation in a more “formal” way. I like to keep notes for myself to better understand where I am in the process and what I'm attempting to implement, so having someone help with cleaning things up in a way that will be useful when I look at the code again after some time was helpful.

- 3: Lastly, for organising the thesis itself. I had no guidance on what things should look like, so I did that in concert with Claude. I also had a PhD thesis and an undergrad thesis I found online for reference, but things like the choice to use “we” in the thesis came out of some tips it gave me on how to sound more academic.

- Finally, I would have it critique my work so I could understand why certain choices are preferred and what options I had. That is how the design decisions appendix came about. This was helpful both in writing and in the actual project development.
